# Step 1: Define the Friction Point

A friction point is a precise, falsifiable description of where something breaks — naming the actor, the action, and where it fails. Spend more time here than feels necessary. The quality of everything downstream depends on it.

---

## Prompt Template

```
I'm trying to solve a problem in [DOMAIN].

The friction is: [DESCRIBE THE PAIN IN ONE TO TWO SENTENCES — what breaks, who feels it, when it happens].

Help me restate this as a single friction point: one sentence, no buzzwords, no solution embedded in the problem statement. The sentence should be falsifiable — a reader should be able to tell whether it is solved or not.

Then tell me what this friction point is NOT, so I can bound the search space.
```

---

## What Good Output Looks Like

A well-formed friction point has three properties:

- **Specific** — names the actor, the action, and where it fails
- **Solution-agnostic** — does not assume the fix
- **Falsifiable** — you can recognize when it is solved

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "We need a better way to manage supply chain data" | "A mid-sized manufacturer cannot identify which tier-2 suppliers are at financial risk until one fails to deliver" |
| "Improve patient experience in clinics" | "Patients wait 15–30 minutes after clinic check-in with no visible progress indicator, producing anxiety even when total wait time is clinically acceptable" |
| "Make our packaging more sustainable" | "Our e-commerce packaging for fragile goods cannot be reduced below 40% air fill without a meaningful increase in transit damage rate" |

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Embedding the solution:** "We need a real-time notification system for…" — that is already a solution choice.
- **Too broad:** "Improve supply chain resilience" — this is a strategic goal, not a friction point.
- **Too vague:** "Customers are frustrated" — with what, specifically?

---

## When to Use

Run this before any patent search. Time spent here is not overhead — it is the search. A vague friction point produces vague results.
